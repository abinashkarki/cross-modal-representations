Representational Geometry Across Language, Vision, and Vision-Language Models

Single-run 22-model study with image-bootstrap uncertainty, source holdout, prompt controls, and aligned-layer analysis under local compute constraints.

Author: Abinash Karki (Independent Research)  |  Date: 2026-03-05  |  Platform: Apple Silicon (M1, 16GB RAM)

Abstract

This paper reports a complete 22-model experiment on a fixed local-hardware budget (Apple M1/16GB) to test what shapes representational geometry across modalities. The final run includes 8 language models, 10 self-supervised vision models, and 4 vision-language models evaluated on 28 concepts (20 base + 8 compounds) with 30 images per concept. We use image-level bootstrap confidence intervals, Mantel permutation tests with BH-FDR, source holdout analysis, prompt-sensitivity controls, and an aligned-layer profile. Across 231 model pairs, median RSA is high within vision (ρ=0.682) and language (ρ=0.661), but low for language-vision pairs (ρ=0.259). Vision-language models are much closer to vision (median ρ=0.702) than to language (median ρ=0.230; 3.06x ratio). Source holdout shows sizable data-conditional movement (LOSO ImageNet mean delta -0.156, max |delta| 0.708), while prompt effects remain non-trivial in language models (mean max-absolute template delta 0.196). In the aligned pass, pairwise mean RSA rises from 0.342 (d00) to 0.464 (d100), yet terminal baseline vs aligned outputs stay nearly unchanged (mean delta +0.0006). We conclude that convergence is modality-asymmetric and dataset-conditional, and that robust controls are required before making strong geometric claims.

Keywords: representational similarity analysis, dataset-conditional geometry, modality asymmetry, source holdout bias, prompt sensitivity, aligned-layer analysis, platonic representation hypothesis.

1. Introduction

The core question of this study is not only whether models converge, but what determines the geometry of that convergence. We evaluate three candidate drivers in one unified run: modality family, architecture depth, and dataset composition in evaluation.

This manuscript treats the experiment as a full standalone study, not as an iteration report. Claims are split into findings and controls so that stronger statements (for example, modality asymmetry and source dependence) are separated from bounding diagnostics (for example, prompt sensitivity and CI width).

2. Experimental Design

2.1 Model Roster and Data

  • Language (8): Qwen3-0.6B/1.7B/4B (MLX q8), Qwen2.5-1.5B, Falcon3-1B, Granite-3.3-2B, LFM2-2.6B, SmolLM3-3B.
  • Vision SSL (10): DINOv2-small/base, ViT-MAE-base, BEiT-base, data2vec-vision, Hiera-base, ConvNeXt-v2, ViT-MSN-base, DINOv3-ConvNeXt-tiny, I-JEPA.
  • Vision-language (4): CLIP-ViT-B32, MetaCLIP-B32-400m, SigLIP, SigLIP2.
  • Concept set: 20 base + 8 compounds; all concepts have exactly 30 images.
  • Source metadata: concept-level source tags + image source mapping support; final source distribution: ImageNet (10), OpenImages (6), Unsplash (12).

2.3 Statistical Protocol

  • Primary representational similarity: Spearman RSA over pairwise concept-similarity vectors.
  • Significance: two-sided Mantel permutation tests (3,000 permutations) with BH-FDR correction.
  • Uncertainty: image-level bootstrap percentile intervals from cached per-image embeddings.
  • Determinism checks: seed-42 rerun identity (except metadata timestamps/output path); seed-change divergence confirmed.
Interpretation guardrail: this design supports dataset-conditional and modality-conditional claims; it does not identify training-data causality because models are not retrained under controlled corpora.

4. Findings vs Ablations

4.1 Primary Findings (Main Claims)

  1. Modality asymmetry is strong: Vision-VLM similarity (median 0.702) is much higher than Language-VLM similarity (median 0.230).
  2. Geometry is dataset-conditional: LOSO shifts are large for some sources (ImageNet mean delta -0.156; max |delta| 0.708).
  3. Depth shapes convergence: aligned-layer means increase from d00=0.3424 to d100=0.4639.
  4. Terminal stability holds: baseline vs aligned final outputs differ minimally (mean delta +0.0006; mean |delta| 0.0007).

4.2 Ablations / Controls (Interpretation Bounds)

  1. Image-bootstrap uncertainty: image-involved pairs show broad CI widths (mean 0.203), so point estimates alone are insufficient.
  2. Prompt sensitivity: language measurements vary by template (mean max-absolute delta 0.196), so single-template claims are fragile.
  3. Source-only coverage limits: openimages/unsplash source-only analyses are skipped by the minimum-concept threshold (<8).
  4. Quantization robustness: q4 sweep is intentionally out of thesis scope and is not used for sign-off claims.

5. Discussion

The results support a constrained interpretation of representational convergence: convergence is strongest within modality and in vision-vision-language coupling, but weak and heterogeneous in language-vision comparisons. This pattern is consistent with partial Platonic convergence and inconsistent with a universal convergence claim at this scale.

The strongest implication is methodological: without image-level resampling and source controls, geometric claims can be overstated. These data support a dataset-conditional interpretation of measured geometry, not a direct causal statement that training data alone dominates architecture.

6. Limitations

  • Source-only evaluation is incomplete for low-concept sources due thresholding (min 8 concepts).
  • MLX language backends expose final hidden state only; aligned-depth for these models collapses to last-layer fallback.
  • Results are bounded to local compute constraints and <=4B core language models.
  • No controlled retraining is performed; architecture-vs-data causality is therefore not identified.
  • Quantization robustness (q4) is intentionally out-of-scope for this thesis version.

8. Conclusion

This study provides a complete, reproducible local-compute evaluation of representational geometry across 22 models and 28 concepts. The main scientific conclusions are: convergence is modality-asymmetric, measured geometry is strongly dataset-conditional, and depth effects are real but do not destabilize terminal outputs. These findings are strong enough for thesis-level claims, with clear bounds from prompt and source-coverage controls.
